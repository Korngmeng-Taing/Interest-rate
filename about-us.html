About This Project

📚 Project Overview

Interest Rate Risk and Bond Pricing under the Dothan Model is an interactive educational platform designed to help students, educators, and financial professionals understand the complexities of interest rate modeling and fixed-income valuation in a stochastic environment.

This comprehensive resource combines:

  • ✅ Rigorous mathematical theory

  • ✅ Practical implementation guidance

  • ✅ Interactive simulations and visualizations

  • ✅ Real-world risk management applications

🎯 Mission Statement

Our mission is to demystify stochastic interest rate models and make them accessible to anyone interested in financial risk management. Through hands-on learning and interactive code examples, we empower students and professionals to:

  • Understand the foundations of interest rate modeling
  • Master Monte Carlo simulation techniques
  • Quantify and manage interest rate risk
  • Apply theoretical concepts to real financial problems

👥 About the Team

Principal Instructor

  • Subject Matter Expert: Financial Risk Management, Quantitative Finance
  • Teaching Approach: Theory-first with practical implementation
  • Specialization: Stochastic modeling, derivative pricing, risk management

Educational Developers

  • Pedagogical guidance and student success

Technical Contributors

  • Python implementation and optimization
  • Quarto website development
  • Data visualization and interactive features

🏆 Why the Dothan Model?

The Dothan Model, proposed by Michael U. Dothan in 1978, remains one of the most elegant and intuitive stochastic interest rate models because:

  1. Simplicity: Geometric Brownian Motion structure is easy to understand
  2. Tractability: Closed-form solutions exist for many problems
  3. Practical Relevance: Used in real financial institutions
  4. Educational Value: Perfect for learning fundamental concepts
  5. Extensibility: Foundation for more complex models (Vasicek, CIR)
